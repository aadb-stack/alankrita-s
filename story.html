Our Story

Born in Pune, woven across India

A house built not on inventory, but on the hands that weave it.

Alankrita — the Sanskrit word for “adorned” — began as one family's love affair with the handloom. What started as quiet trunk-shows for friends in Mayur Colony grew, drape by drape, into a Pune atelier trusted for weaves that are honest about where they come from.

We travel to the loom clusters ourselves — Yeola and Paithan for Paithanis, Kanchipuram for temple-border silks, Varanasi for brocades, Chanderi for whisper-light cottons — and sit with weaver families whose looms have sung for generations. Every saree in our collection is chosen in person, never from a catalogue.

For us, a saree is not stock. It is a promise: that the woman who wears it carries forward a craft, a livelihood, and a story older than any trend. When you drape an Alankrita, you carry a weaver's months of patience on your shoulder.

The Beginning

A handful of trunk-shows

It started in a living room in Mayur Colony — a few Paithanis, a few friends, and the conviction that handwoven deserved better than a dusty shelf.

Going to the Source

Walking into the loom clusters

We stopped buying from agents and started travelling to Yeola, Paithan, Kanchipuram and Varanasi — choosing each weave at the loom that made it.

The Atelier

A home on the 5th floor

Our Pune boutique opened its doors — a calm, unhurried space where patrons could drape, study the zari, and take their time.

Today

A growing family of patrons

From bridal trousseaus to everyday elegance, Alankrita now dresses women across Maharashtra and beyond — each saree still chosen by hand.
